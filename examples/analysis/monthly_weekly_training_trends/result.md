# Result — Monthly and Weekly Training Trends

## Evidence Received

- Eight synthetic activity-grain rows across two fictional calendar months.
- Distance and duration are present in all eight rows.
- Average heart rate is present in seven rows and missing in one.
- Training load is present in seven rows and missing in one.

## Observed Facts

| Month | Activities | Distance | Duration | HR coverage | Load coverage |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| 2030-01 | 4 | 37.0 km | 2.875 h | 3/4 | 4/4 |
| 2030-02 | 4 | 47.0 km | 3.458 h | 4/4 | 3/4 |

Each represented ISO week contains two activities. The sample's total distance
is 84.0 km across eight rows, and total duration is 6.333 hours across eight
rows. February has the same activity count as January, 10.0 km more distance,
and approximately 0.583 more duration hours.

## Interpretation

Within this synthetic sample, frequency is stable while total distance and
duration are higher in the second month. This is a descriptive comparison, not
an explanation of why the change occurred.

## Uncertainty

The sample is small and deliberately constructed. One January heart-rate value
and one February training-load value are missing. No information is available
about recording completeness, terrain, intensity zones, fatigue, or intent.

## Unsupported Conclusions

- Whether fitness improved.
- Whether the second month was safer, harder, or more effective.
- Whether missing values should be treated as zero.
- Whether the pattern represents a real person.

## Possible Next Questions

- Does the activity-type mix explain part of the distance difference?
- Are the same descriptive results present with a longer synthetic period?
- What changes when only running activities are included?
